Why Manage Your Research Data?

As research becomes more data intensive and collaborative, data management is now an important part of responsible conduct of research. A good data management plan both benefits your own project as well as the community. As a result, many funding agencies now require research projects have a data management plan. By managing your data you will:

  • Meet grant requirements and have a more competitive grant application.
  • Increase impact and visibility of your research through more readily achieved research data citation
  • Better guarantee your research data and records are accurate, complete, authentic and reliable.
  • Save time and resources in the long run.
  • Enhance data security and reduce risk of data loss.
  • More easily preserve your research data for the long-term
    • Therefore, it is recommended you create a strategy to organize, preserve, and share your data long after your research concludes.

    Consulting throughout data lifecycle

    Data management is not just important at the end of your research, but is important throughout the lifecycle of your research project. There are many groups and resources on and off campus that can help you write a data management plan and help manage your data throughout its lifecycle.

    Research data presents some unique challenges such as:
    • How do I make my research data always available and usable?
    • How can my peers find and cite my research data?
    • How do I limit access to data initially, and expand access later?
    • How can I share it with only a group of people or institutions?
    • Where do I put my data and how do I get it there?
    • When do I need to start preserving my data?
    • Should I preserve all of my data?
    • How long should I preserve my data?
