Data Management

Sharing & Archiving

Why archive and share your data?

Data management is not just about the plan it is also about executing a strategy that benefits your research objectives and the community. Some reasons to archive and share your data include:

  • It is required by a publisher to share and preserve access to related data
  • Your funding agency requires you to share it. Many funding agencies including NIH and NSF recently instituted guidelines requiring you to share your data.
  • Your research can advance other research in the community
  • Your work is more likely to be cited by others and increase your notoriety within your discipline.
  • The University may require you to preserve your data.

Therefore, it is imperative to be able to archive and share your research quickly and easily.

How can you preserve and share data?

Depending on your needs, there are many options available for archiving and sharing your data both locally and outside Notre Dame. You may also deposit your content locally and link to it from a community repository, or vice versa.

Why Archive your data locally with the Hesburgh Libraries?

  • Your data lives on: Your data is always safe whether you leave the university or not. The Hesburgh Libraries repository will preserve your content as long as needed.
  • Don’t lose your data:You have the peace of mind that your data will have multiple copies with regular integrity checking to ensure no files get corrupted.
  • Stop your data from becoming obsolete: Your data can become unusable if the format becomes obsolete or the software used to open it is no longer available. We will ensure data is migrated and preserved in formats that ensure usability for the long term.
  • Data Citation: Sharing your data, by placing your data in a repository with data citation and
  • Higher Levels of Security: If you need higher levels of security See more under “backups and security” (link here)
  • Easily Share and Publish your data: Opt to share your data with GoogleScholar or other search engines
  • No community repository: There may not be a community based repository suited for your discipline or the concerns of your research project
  • The University owns some or all of the data requiring you to preserve your content locally

Why Archive your data in a community repository?

  • Your community maintains a trusted repository that better meets your sharing needs
  • Archive data alongside that of your peers
  • Your data access needs are not as restrictive
  • Note: You may also want to link to your data in a campus repository to meet both sharing and preservation requirements
