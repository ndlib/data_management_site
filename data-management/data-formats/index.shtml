Data Formats

Some formats make it easier for you to share the data with colleagues. If you use a special system or proprietary data format for collecting/creating your data, you will want to plan to save and store data in that format, but also consider how to convert it to a more open format for storage, archiving, and sharing.

Formats Most easily accessed in the future are:

  • Non-proprietary and not tied to a specific piece of software
  • Open, documented standard
  • In common usage by the research community
  • Standard representation (ASCII, Unicode)
  • Unencrypted
  • Uncompressed
